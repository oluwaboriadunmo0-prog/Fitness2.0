About

Nigerian food and real gym schedules, in one plan

Most meal planners are built around foreign ingredients and portion habits that do not reflect how Nigerian kitchens actually cook. Naija Fuel starts from the other direction: real dishes first, training second, numbers last.

Why it exists

Built from a training kitchen, not a spreadsheet

Naija Fuel began as a way to stop guessing at calories for dishes like egusi soup or ofada rice. Every meal in the library carries a researched estimate for calories and protein, and every exercise in the workout library is chosen for how realistic it is to repeat week after week, with or without a full gym.

The goal is not a perfect diet or a perfect programme. It is a plan you can actually follow on a Tuesday.

What guides it

Three principles

Rooted in Nigerian food

No substitutions for foreign staples. Every dish is one people already cook, priced in calories and protein rather than guesswork.

Built for real gyms

Exercises range from barbell lifts to bodyweight and stairs, so the split still works with limited equipment.

Practical over perfect

A plan you follow at seventy percent beats a perfect one you abandon in week two. Everything here is designed to be repeatable.
